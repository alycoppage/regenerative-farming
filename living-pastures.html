Living Pastures Farm

Jonathan Elliott and his wife recently started Living Pastures in 2019 after getting connected with some people who originally owned the land. The previous owners said they were looking for somebody to farm the land and Elliot decided it was a good match for him and with that, he and his family made the move.

Elliot has previous farming experience as he was previously an employee at another farm.

“I’ve learned a lot, changed a lot of how I’ve done things, and there’s still a lot for me to learn and things that I’d like to keep improving,” Elliot says. “Improvements come through a combination of paying really close attention to how the land responds to our grazing practices, how the animals respond, and then reading and going to conferences and talking with other farmers.”

When it comes to his take on regenerative farming, Elliot says “regenerative is the term people have taken up in order to describe the fact that we need to build back up the soils health to a point where it’s at a stage or a degree.”

Similar to other regenerative farmers, he uses a rotational grazing technique with his cattle. “We’re trying to use the animals to mimic that herd, or flock, grazing pattern that animals are natural because that’s the way in which the soil and the plants and animals have kind of all evolved over the centuries together in a symbiotic relationship,” Elliot said. “The simple way to describe it is basically to constantly move the animals.”

When Elliott utilizes this practice, he’s able to have fresh land constantly and a noticeable difference between grazed and ungrazed land.
